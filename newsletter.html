Geminids Meteor Shower Peaks This Week

Get ready for one of the year's most spectacular celestial shows! The Geminids meteor shower reaches its peak on December 13-14, with up to 120 meteors per hour visible under dark skies. Unlike most meteor showers that originate from comets, the Geminids come from asteroid 3200 Phaethon, making them unique in the astronomical calendar.

Ancient Galaxy Challenges Formation Theories

Astronomers using the James Webb Space Telescope have identified a massive galaxy that existed just 700 million years after the Big Bang. Named CEERS-93316, this galaxy contains roughly one billion solar masses worth of stars, challenging our understanding of how quickly galaxies could form in the early universe. The discovery suggests that galaxy formation was far more efficient than previously thought.
